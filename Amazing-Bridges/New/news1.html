12 beautiful bridges in Europe

Bridges are not just a link between two river banks or two land and cities, but also beautiful masterpieces of engineering and architecture.

Bridge April 25, Lisbon, Portugal: April 25 Bridge is a suspension bridge linking Lisbon, the capital of Portugal, with the city of Almada on the south bank of the Tejo River. The bridge was inaugurated on August 6, 1966, after which a further platform was built in 1999. April 25 Bridge has a total length of 2,277 m, and is the 23rd largest suspension bridge in the world. Until 1974, The bridge was named Salazar Bridge (Ponte Salazar). The name "April 25" is set to commemorate the Revolution Carnation in 1974.

Tower Bridge, London, England:Tower Bridge was built between 1886 and 1894, is a bridge A combination of lifts and suspension bridges, across the River Thames, London. The bridge was built near the Tower of London It was named the Tower Bridge and became the symbol of London.

Ponte Vecchio Bridge, Florence, Italy: The Ponte Vecchio, also known as the Old Bridge, is a medieval arch on the River Arno, in Florence, Italy. The bridge was built with many booths and still exists to this day. Previously, on the bridge mainly There are meat shops, but now there are jewelry shops, souvenirs. Near the Ponte Vecchio Bridge is 2 trees Ponte Santa Trinita and Ponte alle Grazie.

Luis Bridge 1, Porto, Portugal:The Luis Bridge was inaugurated in 1886, 395 meters long and 8 meters wide m, consisting of two overlapping iron ceilings, is considered the symbol of Porto. Visitors can visit the bridge, go Go downstairs to Vila Nova de Gaia or on the floor overlooking the city. Designer The bridge is the engineer Teófilo Seyrig. Today the upper deck of the bridge is the Porto metro line, connecting the area Porto Cathedral with Morro Garden and Avenida Avenue in Vila Nova de Gaia.

Viaduct Millau, Aveyron, France:Via Millau is a cable-stayed bridge spanning the nearby Tarn river valley Millau, southern France. This is the 12th highest bridge in the world, 270 m above the ground, with high masts 343 m. The Viaduct of Millau is part of the axis from Paris to Montpellier, with a construction cost of approximately 470 million USD, officially inaugurated on 14/12/2004 and traffic on 16/12. The bridge has been ranked as one in the great technical achievements of all time.

Bridge of Budapest, Hungary:The Széchenyi Bridge is a bridge over the Danube between Buda and Pest, west and east of Budapest, the capital of Hungary. Bridge designer is British engineer, William Tierney Clark. Széchenyi is the first permanent bridge over the Danube in Hungary, which was inaugurated in 1849.

Rialto Bridge, Venice, Italy:The Rialto Bridge (Ponte di Rialto) is one of four bridges crossing the Grand Canal in Venice, Italy. The Rialto Bridge is the dividing line between San Marco and San Polo and is the oldest bridge crossing the Grand Canal.

Chapel Bridge, Lucerne, Switzerland: Chapel Bridge was built in the first half of the 14th century, is part of the city fort. On the bridge there are paintings hanging from the 17th century that illustrate the beauty of Switzerland and local history, including the history of the saints defending the city. Leodegar and St. Maurice.

Stari Most Old Bridge, Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina:Stari Most was reconstructed from a 16th century Ottoman bridge in the city of Mostar, across the Neretva River and connects two parts of the city. The bridge survived for 427 years, until it was destroyed by Croatian forces on 9 November 1993 during the Croat-Bosniak War. Later, a new bridge was built and opened on July 23, 2004. The bridge is considered one of the most prominent features of the country, as well as the typical Islamic model of the Balkans, designed by Mimar Hayruddin.

Pont de Rohan Bridge, Finistere, France:Pont De Rohan is one of the few bridges in the world. This Landerneau bridge has shops, restaurants and houses and is one of the last man-made bridges built in Europe. This stone bridge was built in 1510, at the behest of the Viscount de Rohan and celebrated its 500th birthday in 2010.

Vasco da Gama Bridge, Lisbon, Portugal:Vasco da Gama Bridge is a cable-stayed bridge, crossing the Tagus River in Parque das Nações in Lisbon, the capital of Portugal. It is the longest bridge in Europe with a total length of 17.2 km. The construction began in February 1995, and the bridge was opened on March 29, 1998, during Expo 98, the 500th anniversary of Vasco da Gama's discovery of sea lanes from Europe to India.

Pont du Gard Bridge, Gard, France:The Pont du Gard is a three-storey bridge leading to ancient Roman water passing through the Gardon River in Vers-Pont-du-Gard near Remoulins in the Gard district of southern France. It was part of the 50 km long Nîmes water pipeline built by the Romans to bring water from a stream into Uzès to the Roman colony of Nîmes. The bridge was built in the 1st century AD and survived almost intact to this day. The Pont du Gard was declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1985.
