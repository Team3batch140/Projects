5 impressive bridges span the Han River in Da Nang

Bridge, Dragon bridge to fly to the sea, Thuan Phuoc hammock bridge ... all across the Han River to create beautiful scenery for Da Nang.

Impressive visitors Da Nang shines bridges
Visitors to Danang are particularly impressed by the unique brilliance that shines brilliantly across the Han River.

Da Nang has long been dubbed "the city of light" by many domestic and international tourists.

This special bridge was designed and built by Vietnamese experts and engineers in the period 1998-2000. The bridge is nearly 500m long and 12m wide, connecting two central roads between Hai Chau district and the district. Son Tra. Everyday at 1am, the center of the bridge turns 90 degrees to open the way for large boats to cross the Han River.

West of Han River is Thuan Phuoc hammock. There are six bridges spanning the Han River including the Han River (also known as the quay), Thuan Phuoc Bridge, Nguyen Van Troi Bridge, Tran Thi Ly Bridge, Dragon Bridge, Tien Son Bridge. Adjacent to Danang Bay, 1.8 km long, 13 m wide, built in over 6 years (2003 - 2009) with a total investment capital of nearly 1,000 billion VND

This bridge across the Han River has a unique architecture with the shape of a dragon reaching out to the sea. The dragon head can fire and water. The bridge was started construction in July 2009 and completed after nearly 4 years of construction, length 666 m and total investment of over 1.700 billion. Dragon Bridge simulates the shape of the dragon in the Ly sea to the East Sea with a span of 568 m in length, weighing nearly 9,000 tons. This is a unique architectural bridge designed by Louis Berger Group (USA), the only steel arch in Southeast Asia.

The Tran Thi Ly Bridge is designed in the shape of a sail on the Han River. The bridge was started in April 2010, 731m long, 34.5m wide and invested more than 1.700 billion. The bridge was inaugurated in March 2013. The bridge has a reinforced concrete pillar with 12 degrees of tilt, with 3 spools and no rigid design such as regular cable-stayed bridges that are rigidly connected to the bridge deck, resting on the S5 shaft through Ball bearings to reduce the size of nails, save materials.

Nguyen Van Troi Bridge has the longest life span, the bridge includes 14 spans of Poni steel length more than 500 m, bridge size 10.5 m, no pedestrian margin. Currently, this bridge is kept as a souvenir of Danang to serve the street walk. The bridge was designed and constructed by RMK (USA) in 1965. The naming of hero Nguyen Van Troi placed the bridge to commemorate the person carrying out the bombing to assassinate US Secretary of Defense McNamara in 1964.
